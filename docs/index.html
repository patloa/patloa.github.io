About meResearchProjects

I am an assistant professor at Toronto Metropolitan University and a member of the Place and Infrastructure Theme of the Bridging Divides program.

My research involves the application of econometric, statistical, and machine learning models to understand travel behaviour and travel demand. Through my research, I aim to gain insights into how shared mobility services and advanced digital technologies can help people meet their mobility needs and access opportunities. I strive to answer research questions that can support the development of policies that contribute to improved mobility, accessibility, and sustainability. My research tends to be collaborative and interdisciplinary in nature, with past projects being completed in collaboration with the City of Toronto, TransLink, and the California Air Resources Board.
